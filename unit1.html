Another skill would be communication, the ability to communicate your point or what you're doing or intend to be doing in a clear, concise and non-superfluous way so that anyone from a layman to a work colleague/team member can respond appropriately, this is key, for example, in presentations, stand ups, and teaching other colleagues what you've learned, a tool I've used for this would be slack, which serves to be an excellent communication hub between myself and teammates, supervisors, etc.

planning and carrying out projects is in and of itself a daunting behemoth of a task, however I find it more appropriate to conceptualise the whole project as an end product, then incrementally break down that end product, then figure out how to make that particular part function, this then becomes a task to do, this works appropriately with the Kanban method mentioned earlier on the trello application

    GDPR is an acronym for General Data Protection Regulation, created by the EU to protect the personal data of anyone considered to be a data subject, (a natural person), and to also keep up to date with the rapid global technological advances, its aim is to adjust the legal regulations regarding the processing and protection of personal data, including identifiers such as a name, identification number, location, or other such factors that specific to physical, physiological, genetic, mental/cognitive, economic, cultural and societal identities, which in current times, there is unprecedented access to and unscrutinized use of personal data by all types of businesses, one of its end goals is to allow data subjects (individuals that use any online service), a significant degree of control over the processing of their data, giving independent public authorities flexible supervisory powers and rights to punish any non compliance via fine or economic sanction. In short, it regulates how companies or businesses store data, and so data flows must be designed with this regulation in mind, therefore must only collect data relevant for their current means, a data minimisation principle, instead of the common method of data retention of collecting and storing great swathes of data because it may be useful to use later. Non compliance with these regulations will incur huge, economically crippling punishments, which will also harm a company's image, not only that, but in cases of Information Technology centred companies, that reside within its member states or outside of the EU, it is required that they submit to an independent authority, such as a data controller or processor who may be forced to take specific actions to remain compliant to the regulation within specific timelines that can usually conflict with business interests, such as temporary limiting on data processing or its outright banning, since its enforcement all companies wishing to do business within the EU/EEA have had to audit personal data flows and define a legal basis for their collection and retention, creating further obligations and expenses on the part of the company.
